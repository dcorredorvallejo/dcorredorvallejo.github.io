Work in Progress

Upskilling Youths or Firms? Randomized Controlled Trial of a Government-led Dual Apprenticeship Program in Nigeria

with Patrick Premand (World Bank), Bruno Crépon (CREST), Oyebola Okunogbe (World Bank), and Nausheen Khan (World Bank) · AEA RCT Registry AEARCTR-0015929 · Endline analysis ongoing; draft in preparation

We study a government-led dual apprenticeship program implemented across six states of Nigeria through a firm-level cluster randomized trial covering roughly 1,370 firms, 1,700 master craftspersons, and 3,700 apprentices. Firms were randomly assigned to receive complementary dual training for apprentices, a skills-upgrading program for master craftspersons, both, or neither — allowing us to compare upskilling youths, upskilling firms, and the value of combining the two. We measure impacts on apprentices’ technical skills, participation in and completion of apprenticeship, employment, and earnings, as well as on the quality of training provided within firms and on firm performance.

I supported this study as a research assistant from the baseline onward and recently joined the research team, now leading the endline analysis work.
