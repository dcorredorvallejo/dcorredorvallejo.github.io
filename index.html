I am a pre-doctoral research assistant at the Development Impact Evaluation (DIME) department of the World Bank, where I support randomized evaluations of apprenticeship, training, and social protection programs in Nigeria, Benin, Côte d'Ivoire, Uganda, and Nicaragua, working with research teams led by Bruno Crépon and Patrick Premand.

My research interests are development economics, labor economics, and the impact evaluation of social programs in low-income settings — in particular, how apprenticeship and training systems shape skills, employment, and earnings for young people.

I am from Bogotá, Colombia. I hold a master's degree in Economics from Universidad de los Andes and a bachelor's degree in Economics from Universidad del Rosario. I am a native Spanish speaker, fluent in English, with intermediate French.

I will be applying to Economics Ph.D. programs in the 2026–27 admissions cycle. You can find my CV here.
